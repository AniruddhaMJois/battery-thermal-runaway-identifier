Project Brief

🔬 Thermal Runaway Detection

This system monitors an external battery cell or load in real-time to detect early signs of thermal runaway. The circuit itself is powered by 2× 3.7V Li-Ion batteries, while sensors watch the target cell for dangerous chain reactions — rising temperature causing further heat generation, leading to fire or explosion.

⚡ IRLZ44N MOSFET — Kill Switch Logic

The IRLZ44N N-Channel MOSFET is used as a high-speed electronic switch for load isolation. When critical thresholds are exceeded, the Arduino drives the MOSFET gate LOW, disconnecting the monitored cell/load in < 1ms — far faster than any mechanical relay. Its logic-level gate threshold (1.0–2.0V) allows direct drive from Arduino's 5V GPIO.

🧪 MQ-2 — Early Chemical Venting Detection

The MQ-2 gas sensor detects volatile organic compounds and electrolyte vapors released during the early stages of cell degradation — before temperature spikes occur. This provides a critical early-warning layer that pure thermal monitoring cannot achieve, enabling preemptive isolation.

🏗️ System Architecture

Power: 2× 3.7V Li-Ion batteries → Arduino UNO. Sensors: MQ-2 (Analog A0) + LM35/Thermistor (Analog A1). Kill Switch: IRLZ44N MOSFET Gate (Digital Pin 7) → Monitored Cell/Load Isolation. Data transmitted via USB Serial at 9600 baud.
